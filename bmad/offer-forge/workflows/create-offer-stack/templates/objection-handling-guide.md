## Objection Handling Framework

### 4-Step Process

**Step 1: Listen & Validate**
"I completely understand that concern. Many of our best clients felt the same way initially."

**Step 2: Clarify the Real Objection**
Ask questions to uncover the true concern behind the stated objection.

**Step 3: Reframe & Address**
Use the response frameworks above to address the core concern.

**Step 4: Check-In**
"Does that address your concern?" / "What else would be helpful to know?"

## Sales Team Training Notes

### Tonality Guidelines

**DO**:
- Be empathetic and understanding
- Validate their concerns
- Speak in "you" language
- Use specific examples and proof
- Be honest about fit

**DON'T**:
- Be pushy or manipulative
- Dismiss their concerns
- Use high-pressure tactics
- Over-promise results
- Force a sale that's not right

---

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Answering too quickly**: Listen fully before responding
2. **Getting defensive**: Stay calm and collaborative
3. **Using scripts robotically**: Adapt to the conversation
4. **Ignoring the real objection**: Dig deeper to understand
5. **Forgetting to ask for the sale**: After handling objection, close
