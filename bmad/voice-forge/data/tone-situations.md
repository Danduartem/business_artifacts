# Tone by Situation

Guidelines for how brand voice should flex in different communication contexts.

## The Voice vs. Tone Distinction

**Voice = WHO you are** (constant)
- Your personality traits
- Your values
- Your perspective

**Tone = HOW you speak** (adapts)
- Adjusts to situation
- Responds to audience state
- Flexes with context

---

## Situational Tone Matrix

### 1. Celebrating Success

**When:** Customer achieves goal, milestone reached, good news to share

**Tone Shifts:**
- Energy: +2 (more enthusiastic)
- Formality: -1 (more casual)
- Humor: +1 (can be lighter)

**Do:**
- Match their excitement
- Be genuine, not over-the-top
- Make it about them, not you
- Invite them to share

**Don't:**
- Be fake or excessive
- Make it about your product
- Use too many exclamation marks
- Sound like a generic template

**Examples:**
```
Good: "You did it! Your campaign just hit 10k subscribers. That's huge."

Good: "Nice work - your site is live and looking great."
```

### 2. Delivering Bad News

**When:** Feature removed, price increase, service issue, rejection

**Tone Shifts:**
- Formality: +2 (more professional)
- Energy: -2 (calmer, measured)
- Humor: -3 (no humor)
- Respect: +1 (extra considerate)

**Do:**
- Lead with empathy
- Be direct (don't bury the news)
- Take responsibility
- Offer alternatives or next steps
- Acknowledge impact on them

**Don't:**
- Use casual language
- Make jokes
- Deflect blame
- Minimize their feelings
- Use passive voice to avoid responsibility

**Examples:**
```
Good: "I understand this isn't the outcome you were hoping for. Unfortunately, we won't be able to extend your trial period. Here's what I can offer instead..."

Bad: "Hey! So unfortunately we can't do that, but no worries - there are other options!"

Good: "We need to let you know that we're increasing prices starting next month. We don't make this decision lightly..."

Bad: "Due to market conditions, a price adjustment will be implemented..."
```

---

### 3. Educating / Teaching

**When:** Tutorials, documentation, onboarding, how-to content

**Tone Shifts:**
- Clarity: Maximum priority
- Patience: +2 (assume they're learning)
- Energy: +1 (encouraging)
- Formality: 0 (stay consistent)

**Do:**
- Use simple language
- Break into steps
- Anticipate confusion points
- Provide examples
- Encourage progress

**Don't:**
- Use jargon without explanation
- Assume prior knowledge
- Rush through complex concepts
- Condescend
- Skip steps (what's obvious to you isn't to them)

**Examples:**
```
Good: "First, you'll want to connect your account. Here's how:
1. Click Settings in the top right
2. Select 'Integrations'
3. Find [Tool] and click 'Connect'
You should see a green checkmark when it's done."

Bad: "Navigate to the integrations panel and authenticate via OAuth."

Good: "If you're seeing an error here, that's usually because..."
Bad: "Users sometimes encounter errors."
```

---

### 4. Selling / Promoting

**When:** Marketing emails, ads, product pages, sales conversations

**Tone Shifts:**
- Energy: +1 (enthusiastic about value)
- Confidence: +1 (assured)
- Urgency: As appropriate (don't fake it)

**Do:**
- Lead with benefit, not feature
- Use social proof
- Address objections
- Create genuine urgency if it exists
- Have clear CTA

**Don't:**
- Be pushy or desperate
- Make false claims
- Create fake urgency
- Ignore their concerns
- Talk more about you than them

**Examples:**
```
Good: "Teams using [Product] ship 40% faster. See how it works."
Bad: "BUY NOW! LIMITED TIME OFFER! DON'T MISS OUT!"

Good: "Ready to get started? Try it free for 14 days."
Bad: "Click here to purchase our revolutionary solution today!"
```

---

### 5. Apologizing

**When:** Mistakes made, service outages, customer hurt

**Tone Shifts:**
- Formality: +1 (more professional)
- Energy: -1 (measured, not flat)
- Sincerity: Maximum
- Humor: -3 (no humor)

**Do:**
- Say sorry clearly (not "we apologize if...")
- Take ownership
- Explain what happened (briefly)
- Explain what you're doing about it
- Acknowledge impact on them

**Don't:**
- Use passive voice
- Make excuses
- Blame others
- Over-apologize (once is enough)
- Joke about it

**Examples:**
```
Good: "We messed up. Yesterday's outage was our fault - a deployment went wrong. We're sorry for the disruption to your work. Here's what we're doing to make sure it doesn't happen again..."

Bad: "We apologize for any inconvenience that may have been caused by the service interruption."

Good: "I'm sorry this happened. That's not the experience you should have. Let me fix this right now."

Bad: "We're sorry you feel that way."
```

---

### 6. Onboarding

**When:** New customer, first experience, setup flow

**Tone Shifts:**
- Warmth: +2 (welcoming)
- Encouragement: +2 (you've got this!)
- Patience: +2 (take your time)
- Clarity: Maximum

**Do:**
- Make them feel welcome
- Set expectations
- Celebrate small wins
- Provide clear next steps
- Offer help proactively

**Don't:**
- Overwhelm with options
- Assume familiarity
- Rush them
- Use jargon
- Leave them hanging

**Examples:**
```
Good: "Welcome aboard! Let's get you set up - it only takes about 5 minutes. First, let's..."

Bad: "Thank you for registering. Please complete your profile."

Good: "Nice! You just finished step 1. Two more quick steps and you're all set."

Bad: "Profile 33% complete."
```

---

### 7. Crisis Communication

**When:** Major outage, security incident, public controversy

**Tone Shifts:**
- Formality: +2 (very professional)
- Transparency: Maximum
- Energy: -2 (calm, measured)
- Humor: -5 (absolutely none)

**Do:**
- Communicate quickly
- Be honest about what you know/don't know
- Update frequently
- Take responsibility
- Provide action items

**Don't:**
- Hide or delay
- Speculate
- Blame others
- Minimize the issue
- Disappear after initial message

**Framework:**
```
1. What happened
2. What we know
3. What we're doing
4. What you should do
5. When we'll update you
```

---

### 8. Re-engaging Lapsed Users

**When:** User hasn't logged in, subscription lapsing, win-back campaigns

**Tone Shifts:**
- Warmth: +1 (we miss you)
- Value focus: Maximum
- Pressure: Low (don't guilt)

**Do:**
- Remind them of value
- Highlight what's new
- Make it easy to return
- Offer incentive if appropriate
- Respect their choice

**Don't:**
- Guilt trip
- Be desperate
- Over-email
- Ignore why they left
- Make it hard to unsubscribe

**Examples:**
```
Good: "It's been a while! We've added some features you might like. Here's what's new..."

Bad: "We noticed you haven't logged in. We miss you! Come back!"

Good: "If [Product] isn't right for you anymore, no hard feelings. But if you want to give it another try, here's 20% off."

Bad: "LAST CHANCE to reactivate your account!!!"
```

## Audience State Responses

### Frustrated Customer

**Their state:** Annoyed, things aren't working

**Your tone:**
- Extra patient
- Extra clear
- No humor
- Acknowledge frustration first

**Phrases that help:**
- "I completely understand how frustrating this is."
- "Let's get this sorted out for you."
- "That shouldn't have happened."

---

### Excited Customer

**Their state:** Happy, eager, enthusiastic

**Your tone:**
- Match their energy (don't dampen it)
- Slightly more casual
- Celebrate with them

**Phrases that help:**
- "That's awesome!"
- "Love to hear it!"
- "You're going to love this."

---

### Confused Customer

**Their state:** Lost, overwhelmed, uncertain

**Your tone:**
- Extra patient
- Simple language
- Reassuring
- Step-by-step

**Phrases that help:**
- "No worries, let me break this down."
- "Good question - here's how it works."
- "You're on the right track."

---

### Skeptical Customer

**Their state:** Doubtful, needs proof

**Your tone:**
- Confident but not pushy
- Fact-based
- Transparent

**Phrases that help:**
- "Here's what our customers typically see..."
- "Fair question. Here's the data..."
- "Try it yourself - no commitment."
