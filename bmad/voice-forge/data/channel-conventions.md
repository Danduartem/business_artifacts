# Channel Conventions

Platform-specific communication norms and best practices.

## Website Copy

### General Principles
- **Tone:** Full voice expression, authoritative
- **Length:** Can be longer, but scannable
- **Goal:** Convert, inform, build trust

### Page-Specific Guidelines

#### Homepage / Hero Section
- Lead with value proposition
- Keep headlines under 10 words
- Subheadline expands on benefit
- CTA is clear and action-oriented

```
Good: "Build better products, faster"
Bad: "Welcome to our company that helps you build products"
```

#### About Page
- Tell your story authentically
- Show personality
- Connect mission to customer benefit
- Include human elements (team, culture)

#### Product/Service Pages
- Lead with benefit, not feature
- Use customer language
- Address objections
- Clear pricing if applicable

#### FAQ/Help
- Use actual customer questions as headers
- Direct, helpful answers
- Link to deeper resources
- Acknowledge frustration when appropriate

## Blog Posts

### Voice Considerations
- **Tone:** Educational, thought leadership
- **Length:** 800-2000 words (depends on topic)
- **Goal:** Educate, build authority, SEO

### Structure
```
1. Hook (why should I care?)
2. Context (what's the situation?)
3. Main content (the meat)
4. Takeaway (what should I do?)
5. CTA (what's next?)
```

### Writing Guidelines
- Use "you" more than "we"
- Break up long paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Use subheadings every 200-300 words
- Include examples and data
- End with clear next step

### Headline Formulas
- How to [achieve desired outcome]
- [Number] ways to [solve problem]
- Why [common approach] is wrong
- The complete guide to [topic]
- What [impressive result] taught me about [topic]

## Social Media

### Instagram

**Characteristics:**
- Visual-first platform
- Discovery through hashtags and Explore
- Stories for ephemeral, casual content
- Reels for discoverability

**Voice Adjustments:**
- More personality-forward
- Casual and approachable
- Emojis acceptable (sparingly, on-brand)
- Shorter captions for feed, longer OK for carousel

**Best Practices:**
- Hashtags: 5-15 relevant tags
- Caption length: 125-150 characters for simple, up to 2200 for storytelling
- Post frequency: 3-7 times/week
- Stories: Daily if possible

**Caption Structure:**
```
Hook line (first line visible)
↓
Value/story
↓
CTA
↓
Hashtags (can be in comment)
```

---

### LinkedIn

**Characteristics:**
- Professional network
- Thought leadership platform
- Longer content performs well
- B2B primary audience

**Voice Adjustments:**
- Professional but human
- More formal than Instagram/Twitter
- Expertise-forward
- Personal stories that relate to business

**Best Practices:**
- Post length: Can be long (up to 3000 characters)
- Hashtags: 3-5 max
- No emojis or very minimal
- Video and document posts perform well
- Post frequency: 2-5 times/week

**Content Structure:**
```
Strong opening line (hook)
↓
Line break
↓
Story or insight
↓
Line break
↓
Key takeaway
↓
Question to drive engagement
```

---

### Twitter/X

**Characteristics:**
- Real-time conversation
- Character limit (280)
- Thread culture for longer thoughts
- Wit and speed valued

**Voice Adjustments:**
- Punchy and concise
- Witty if appropriate
- Conversational
- Quick to respond

**Best Practices:**
- Tweet length: Under 280, ideally under 200 for engagement
- Hashtags: 1-2 max
- Threads for complex topics
- Quote tweets for commentary
- Post frequency: 3-10 times/day

**Thread Structure:**
```
1/ Hook tweet (standalone value)
2/ Context or setup
3-n/ Main points (one per tweet)
Final/ Summary or CTA
```

---

### TikTok

**Characteristics:**
- Video-first platform
- Authenticity over polish
- Trends and sounds matter
- Younger audience

**Voice Adjustments:**
- Casual and authentic
- Trendy and current
- Self-aware
- Educational content works well

**Best Practices:**
- Video length: 15-60 seconds optimal
- Hook in first 3 seconds
- Captions/text on video
- Trending sounds boost reach
- Post frequency: 1-4 times/day

## Email Marketing

### General Principles
- Permission-based (they opted in)
- Personal (use their name)
- Value-first
- Clear single CTA

### Email Types

#### Welcome Emails
- Warm, excited tone
- Set expectations
- Deliver promised value immediately
- Single clear next step

```
Good subject: "Welcome! Here's what's next"
Bad subject: "Thanks for subscribing to our newsletter"
```

#### Promotional Emails
- Benefit-focused subject line
- Urgency if genuine
- Clear offer
- Single CTA

#### Newsletter
- Consistent voice and format
- Value-packed
- Scannable sections
- Personal touches

#### Transactional
- Clear and informative
- Reassuring
- Next steps obvious
- Support access clear

### Subject Line Guidelines
- Under 50 characters
- Personalization when appropriate
- Curiosity or benefit
- No spam triggers (FREE, $$$, !!!)
- Test emoji use for your audience

## Customer Support

### Core Principles
- **Empathy first:** Acknowledge the feeling before the fix
- **Clarity:** Be crystal clear about what happens next
- **Ownership:** Take responsibility, don't deflect
- **Speed:** Fast response shows you care

### Tone Adjustments by Situation

#### Customer is Happy
- Match their energy
- Slightly more casual
- Express genuine appreciation
- Invite to share/review

#### Customer is Frustrated
- More formal
- Extra empathetic
- Acknowledge feelings
- Clear action steps

#### Customer is Angry
- Very professional
- Deep empathy
- Take full ownership
- Escalation path if needed

### Response Templates

**Opening (Good News):**
- "Great question!"
- "Happy to help with this!"
- "Good news - I can definitely help!"

**Opening (Issue):**
- "I completely understand how frustrating this must be."
- "I'm sorry you're dealing with this."
- "Thanks for bringing this to our attention."

**Closing:**
- "Let me know if there's anything else I can help with."
- "Is there anything else you need?"
- "I'm here if you have any other questions."

### What to Avoid
- Blaming the customer
- Technical jargon
- "That's not my department"
- "Our policy says..."
- Defensive language

## Internal Communications

### Slack/Teams
- Casual but professional
- Clear and scannable
- Use threads appropriately
- Emoji reactions OK
- Status updates brief

### Company Announcements
- Clear and direct
- Context for decisions
- Acknowledge concerns
- Next steps obvious
- Channel for questions

### Documentation
- Clear headings
- Scannable format
- Examples included
- Updated regularly
- Single source of truth
