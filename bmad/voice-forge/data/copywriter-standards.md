# Universal Copywriter Standards

> **Purpose:** These rules ensure all generated voice documentation produces human-authentic copy that avoids common AI and course-marketing patterns.

---

## Core Principles

These standards apply to ALL voice documentation generated by Voice Forge, regardless of brand personality, industry, or target audience.

---

## 1. Punctuation: No Decorative Dashes

### Rule
**NEVER use em-dashes (—) or decorative hyphens (-) to break up sentences.**

### Why
Em-dashes and decorative hyphens are a telltale sign of AI-generated text. Human copywriters typically use:
- Periods (creating short, punchy sentences)
- Commas (for natural pauses)
- Colons (for introductions)
- Parentheses (for asides)

### Examples

| Bad (AI Pattern) | Good (Human Pattern) |
|------------------|---------------------|
| "Marketing that works — not that wastes money" | "Marketing that works. Not that wastes money." |
| "We connect all your channels — social, email, ads — into one system" | "We connect all your channels (social, email, ads) into one system" |
| "Results first — always" | "Results first. Always." |

### Implementation
- All generated examples must avoid em-dashes
- Use periods for emphasis breaks
- Use commas for natural flow
- Use parentheses for clarifying lists
- Use colons to introduce lists or explanations

---

## 2. Gender: No Parenthetical Gender Markers

### Rule
**NEVER use (a), (o), or similar gender markers. Always choose a defined gender.**

### Why
Parenthetical gender markers like "empreendedor(a)" look automated and unnatural. Real copywriters choose one form based on:
- The brand avatar's gender
- The primary audience gender
- Masculine as default for mixed audiences (in Portuguese)

### Examples

| Bad (Automated) | Good (Human) |
|-----------------|--------------|
| "Caro(a) cliente" | "Cara cliente" (if avatar is female) |
| "Empreendedor(a)" | "Empreendedora" (if targeting women) |
| "Pronto(a) para começar?" | "Pronta para começar?" |

### Implementation
- Ask during brand context gathering: "What is the gender of your brand avatar or primary audience?"
- Default to masculine for mixed/unknown audiences
- Be consistent throughout all documentation

## 3. Prohibited Words

### Rule
**NEVER use these words in any context:**

| Word | Why Prohibited |
|------|----------------|
| **gratuito** | Associated with cheap course marketing |
| **grátis** | Same as above, feels desperate |
| **Pix** | Payment method that cheapens brand perception |

### Alternative Approaches
Instead of emphasizing "free," focus on:
- Value delivery without price focus
- "Sem compromisso" (no commitment) if needed
- Simply omit the word: "Baixe o diagnóstico" instead of "Baixe o diagnóstico gratuito"

### Examples

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| "Diagnóstico gratuito" | "Diagnóstico" or "Diagnóstico sem compromisso" |
| "Checklist gratuita" | "Checklist" or "Checklist completa" |
| "Pague via Pix" | "Formas de pagamento disponíveis" |

---

## 4. Headlines: No Sensationalist Transformation Claims

### Rule
**NEVER use "before → after" transformation headlines that sound like course marketing.**

### Prohibited Patterns
- "Do X à Y" (e.g., "Do burnout à liberdade")
- "De X para Y" (e.g., "De funcionário para milionário")
- Extreme transformation claims
- "Vida nova" or "transforme sua vida" type claims

### Why
These patterns are extremely overused in predatory course marketing. They:
- Trigger skepticism in sophisticated audiences
- Sound like empty promises
- Position the brand as a "guru"

### Better Approaches
- Focus on specific, measurable results
- Use concrete numbers when possible
- Describe the mechanism, not just the outcome

### Examples

| Bad (Sensationalist) | Good (Specific) |
|---------------------|-----------------|
| "Do burnout à liberdade" | "Sistema que vende enquanto você dorme" |
| "De 0 a 100k/mês" | "Aumento médio de 3.2x no faturamento" |
| "Transforme sua vida" | "Recupere 15 horas por semana" |

---

## 5. Uniqueness Claims: No "The Only" Positioning

### Rule
**NEVER use "a única", "o único", or similar exclusive claims.**

### Prohibited Phrases
- "A única forma de..."
- "O único método que..."
- "Somos os únicos..."
- "A única solução..."

### Why
These claims are:
- Almost always false (there are always alternatives)
- Extremely overused by course sellers
- Red flags for sophisticated buyers
- Often violate advertising standards

### Better Approaches
- Focus on what makes you different (not "only")
- Use specific evidence and results
- Let differentiation speak through specifics

### Examples

| Bad (Cliché) | Good (Specific) |
|--------------|-----------------|
| "A única agência que entrega ROI" | "Agência focada 100% em ROI (sem métricas de vaidade)" |
| "O único método comprovado" | "Método validado com 50+ empresas" |
| "A única forma de escalar" | "Sistema de escala com 90% de taxa de sucesso" |

## Quality Checklist Integration

When scoring voice documentation, verify:

- [ ] **No em-dashes (—)** or decorative hyphens in examples
- [ ] **No (a)/(o) gender markers** - consistent gender choice made
- [ ] **No prohibited words**: gratuito, grátis, Pix
- [ ] **No sensationalist headlines**: "do X à Y" patterns absent
- [ ] **No "única/único" claims**: exclusivity claims removed
