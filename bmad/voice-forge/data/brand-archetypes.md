# Brand Archetypes

Based on Carl Jung's psychological archetypes, adapted for brand voice development.

## The 12 Brand Archetypes

Each archetype has distinct voice characteristics, vocabulary preferences, and communication styles.

---

### 1. The Innocent

**Core Desire:** To be happy
**Goal:** To experience paradise
**Fear:** Doing something wrong or bad
**Strategy:** Do things right

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Optimistic and positive
- Simple and clear language
- Trustworthy and honest
- Wholesome without being naive

**Vocabulary:**
- Use: pure, simple, honest, natural, good, happy, safe, trust
- Avoid: complex, edgy, controversial, risky

**Tone:** Warm, sincere, straightforward

**Brand Examples:** Coca-Cola (classic), Dove, Whole Foods

---

### 2. The Explorer

**Core Desire:** Freedom to find out who you are
**Goal:** To experience a more authentic, fulfilling life
**Fear:** Getting trapped, conformity
**Strategy:** Journey, seek out new things

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Adventurous and bold
- Independent and individualistic
- Inspiring wanderlust
- Anti-establishment undertones

**Vocabulary:**
- Use: discover, journey, explore, freedom, authentic, adventure, pioneer, path
- Avoid: settle, routine, ordinary, safe

**Tone:** Inspiring, adventurous, independent

**Brand Examples:** Jeep, Patagonia, REI, GoPro

---

### 3. The Sage

**Core Desire:** To find the truth
**Goal:** To use intelligence to understand the world
**Fear:** Being duped, ignorance
**Strategy:** Seek out information and knowledge

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Knowledgeable and authoritative
- Educational without being condescending
- Thoughtful and analytical
- Evidence-based

**Vocabulary:**
- Use: understand, learn, know, discover, insight, wisdom, truth, research
- Avoid: believe, feel, guess, maybe

**Tone:** Intelligent, informative, credible

**Brand Examples:** Google, TED, Harvard, The Economist

---

### 4. The Hero

**Core Desire:** To prove one's worth through courage
**Goal:** Exert mastery to improve the world
**Fear:** Weakness, vulnerability
**Strategy:** Become strong and competent

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Motivating and empowering
- Confident and bold
- Action-oriented
- Challenging the status quo

**Vocabulary:**
- Use: achieve, conquer, win, courage, strength, power, overcome, champion
- Avoid: weak, fail, can't, impossible

**Tone:** Inspiring, bold, determined

**Brand Examples:** Nike, Under Armour, FedEx, BMW

---

### 5. The Outlaw (Rebel)

**Core Desire:** Revenge or revolution
**Goal:** To overturn what isn't working
**Fear:** Being powerless or ineffectual
**Strategy:** Disrupt, destroy, shock

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Provocative and bold
- Rule-breaking and unconventional
- Edgy and sometimes controversial
- Anti-establishment

**Vocabulary:**
- Use: rebel, revolution, break, disrupt, change, defy, challenge, radical
- Avoid: traditional, conventional, safe, normal

**Tone:** Bold, provocative, unapologetic

**Brand Examples:** Harley-Davidson, Virgin, Diesel

---

### 6. The Magician

**Core Desire:** Understanding fundamental laws of the universe
**Goal:** To make dreams come true
**Fear:** Unintended negative consequences
**Strategy:** Develop a vision and live by it

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Visionary and transformative
- Mysterious and intriguing
- Inspiring wonder
- Making the impossible possible

**Vocabulary:**
- Use: transform, magic, dream, imagine, vision, create, believe, possible
- Avoid: ordinary, realistic, practical, impossible

**Tone:** Inspiring, mystical, visionary

**Brand Examples:** Apple, Disney, Tesla, Dyson

---

### 7. The Regular Guy/Gal (Everyman)

**Core Desire:** Connection with others
**Goal:** To belong
**Fear:** Being left out or standing out
**Strategy:** Develop ordinary solid virtues

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Relatable and down-to-earth
- Friendly and approachable
- Unpretentious
- Inclusive

**Vocabulary:**
- Use: everyone, together, real, honest, straightforward, simple, friendly
- Avoid: exclusive, elite, fancy, special

**Tone:** Friendly, humble, relatable

**Brand Examples:** IKEA, Target, Budweiser, eBay

---

### 8. The Lover

**Core Desire:** Intimacy and experience
**Goal:** Being in relationship with people, work, experiences
**Fear:** Being alone, unwanted
**Strategy:** Become more attractive

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Sensual and passionate
- Emotionally expressive
- Intimate and personal
- Appreciating beauty

**Vocabulary:**
- Use: love, passion, beauty, desire, pleasure, experience, feel, indulge
- Avoid: cold, logical, practical, efficient

**Tone:** Warm, passionate, intimate

### 9. The Jester

**Core Desire:** To live in the moment with full enjoyment
**Goal:** To have a great time and lighten up the world
**Fear:** Being bored or boring others
**Strategy:** Play, make jokes, be funny

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Playful and fun
- Irreverent and witty
- Not taking themselves seriously
- Entertaining

**Vocabulary:**
- Use: fun, play, laugh, enjoy, crazy, awesome, hilarious, party
- Avoid: serious, important, formal, professional

**Tone:** Playful, witty, irreverent

**Brand Examples:** Old Spice, M&Ms, Skittles, Geico

---

### 10. The Caregiver

**Core Desire:** To protect and care for others
**Goal:** To help others
**Fear:** Selfishness and ingratitude
**Strategy:** Do things for others

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Nurturing and supportive
- Generous and compassionate
- Reassuring
- Service-oriented

**Vocabulary:**
- Use: care, help, support, protect, nurture, comfort, safe, trust
- Avoid: selfish, alone, independent, compete

**Tone:** Warm, supportive, compassionate

**Brand Examples:** Johnson & Johnson, TOMS, Volvo, Campbell's

---

### 11. The Creator

**Core Desire:** To create things of enduring value
**Goal:** To realize a vision
**Fear:** Mediocre vision or execution
**Strategy:** Develop artistic control and skill

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Innovative and imaginative
- Artistic and expressive
- Non-conformist
- Perfectionist

**Vocabulary:**
- Use: create, design, imagine, build, craft, innovate, original, vision
- Avoid: copy, ordinary, standard, template

**Tone:** Inspiring, innovative, artistic

**Brand Examples:** LEGO, Adobe, Apple, Crayola

---

### 12. The Ruler

**Core Desire:** Control
**Goal:** Create a prosperous, successful family or community
**Fear:** Chaos, being overthrown
**Strategy:** Exercise power

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Authoritative and commanding
- Confident and assured
- Premium and exclusive
- Setting the standard

**Vocabulary:**
- Use: lead, premium, exclusive, power, authority, control, best, superior
- Avoid: budget, ordinary, follower, alternative

**Tone:** Confident, authoritative, refined

**Brand Examples:** Mercedes-Benz, Rolex, American Express, Microsoft

## Using Archetypes for Voice Development

### Primary + Secondary

Most brands have:
- **Primary archetype** (dominant voice, 70%)
- **Secondary archetype** (supporting nuances, 30%)

Example: Apple = Magician (primary) + Creator (secondary)

### Archetype Combinations

Some natural pairings:
- Sage + Creator = Thought leadership
- Hero + Explorer = Adventure brand
- Caregiver + Innocent = Trust brand
- Jester + Regular Guy = Approachable fun

Unlikely pairings (creates tension):
- Ruler + Jester
- Outlaw + Caregiver
- Innocent + Outlaw

### Questions to Identify Your Archetype

1. What does your brand help people become?
2. What would your brand fight against?
3. How does your brand make people feel?
4. If your brand was a person at a party, how would they act?
5. What kind of story does your brand tell?
